§ The exercise

A guided red-teaming exercise.

In Part III you'll work in small mixed groups (technical and policy researchers together) to apply the methodology to a focused red-teaming exercise. Facilitators circulate to coach the work and push prompt construction.

Bring a laptop. Materials and facilitation guides are provided on the day.

§ Responsible conduct

The exercise teaches a method, not how to generate harm.

Materials prepared in advance. Voluntary, 18+, written consent at the start. No personally identifying information collected; prompts documented without attribution and never reproduced verbatim.

§ FAQ

Common questions.

  • Do I need to know how to red-team already? +
    No prior experience is required. The workshop is designed to introduce the methodology from scratch, with facilitators guiding you throughout the hands-on exercise.
  • Will I leave able to red-team independently? +
    You'll leave with a solid working understanding of the method and have completed a full red-teaming sequence. Independent practice will develop further with time, and we'll share resources to support that.
  • I work in policy and don't code — is this for me? +
    Yes. The workshop is explicitly designed for mixed technical and non-technical audiences. Groups are intentionally mixed, and the methodology does not require coding skills.
  • How do I sign up? +
    Registration details will be announced alongside the Deep Learning Indaba 2026 programme. Check back here or follow CASA and UCT AI Initiative for updates.
